HARD MCQ

A 68-year-old male with CKD Stage 3 requires DOAC therapy for non-valvular atrial fibrillation. Which agent requires the most significant dose adjustment based on renal function?

school

Clinical Rationale

Dabigatran is predominantly cleared by the kidneys (~80%). In CKD Stage 3 (CrCl 15–30 mL/min), the dosage must be reduced to 75 mg twice daily.

Key Rule: Apixaban has significantly less renal clearance (~27%) and uses the ABC criteria (Age >80, Body weight <60kg, Creatinine >1.5) for dose adjustment — not CrCl alone.

    Pharmacokinetics Has Note

    ID: Q-8824

    Saved: Oct 12

    A 65-year-old male with CKD Stage 3 requires DOAC therapy for non-valvular atrial fibrillation. Which agent requires the most significant dose adjustment based on renal function?

    Your Answer (Incorrect)

    Apixaban

    Correct Answer

    Dabigatran

    Clinical Rationale

    Dabigatran is predominantly cleared by the kidneys (~80%). In CKD Stage 3 with CrCl 15–30 mL/min, the dosage must be reduced to 75 mg twice daily. Apixaban uses the ABC criteria, not CrCl alone.

    edit_note
    editPersonal Note

    "Always check the 80% rule for Dabigatran. Apixaban relies on the ABC criteria (Age >80, BW <60kg, Cr >1.5). Don't mix up the clearance pathways during exams."

    Infectious Disease

    ID: Q-9105

    Saved: Sep 28

    Evaluate the interaction between Linezolid and SSRIs. What is the primary mechanistic concern?

    Correct Answer

    Serotonin Syndrome due to non-selective MAO inhibition by Linezolid.

    Clinical Rationale

    Linezolid possesses weak, reversible, nonselective MAO inhibitory activity. Concurrent use with serotonergic agents like SSRIs increases the risk of serotonin syndrome.
